Title: A message from Buckingham Palace
Date: 25 Jun 2026

My loyal subjects, 

Over the course of the past month, there have been two attempts to initiate a vote of no confidence against the Prime Minister. Such repeated motions represent a grave and improper use of the authority entrusted to Members by the people whom they are sworn to serve.

For more than half a millennium, the Crown has refrained from direct intervention in the proceedings of Parliament, respecting the constitutional balance that has long defined our nation. Yet the present climate of ceaseless contest and obstruction risks diminishing the dignity and effectiveness of this House, and threatens to leave this great nation lagging behind where unity and resolve are required.

Let it therefore be known: While I do not, at this moment, seek the dissolution of this Parliament. I issue a caution to those who sit within it. Govern with prudence, with restraint, and with fidelity to the people.

For while the Crown endures in patience, the temper of the people is not inexhaustible. A sovereign’s first duty is to the stability and welfare of the realm, and I am ever mindful of the sentiments of our subjects.

Proceed, therefore, with caution. The Crown observes.
