Title: The Manchester Evening News will now publish exclusively in Mandarin
Date: 31 January 2027

After legislation by the Manchester City Council passed stating that all Fish and Chips shops will be replaced with Haidilao Hotpot, and that the primary medium of language in the city will be in Chinese, the Manchester Evening News will be publishing in Mandarin from hereon.

Editor-in-chief Daniel Cartwright reassured audiences in a press conference that the publication remains committed to local journalism. “We’re still covering the same stories, council meetings, United versus City, suspiciously aggressive pigeons in Piccadilly Gardens, just in a different language,” he explained, before unveiling the paper’s new name: 曼彻斯特晚报 (Mànchèsītè Wǎnbào).

Right-wing leader Tommy Robinson has expressed his disgust at the statement and hopes that his colleagues in Parliament will work to deport all the Chinese people in the UK.
