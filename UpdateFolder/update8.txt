Title: Attempt to overthrow the Prime Minister
Date: 25 May 2026

Moments ago, an unlikely coalition of extremist factions from both the far-left and far-right in the House of Commons attempted to remove Prime Minister Keir Starmer from office.

A vote of no confidence was brought before Parliament. In a surprising move, the Prime Minister himself voted in favour of holding the vote. Many analysts believe this may have been a strategic decision intended to reaffirm his mandate and demonstrate confidence in his leadership.

The strategy appeared to work. Prime Minister Starmer ultimately retained his position after securing the support of the majority of Members of Parliament. However, observers noted a small fracture within his own ranks, as two delegates from his council voted against him.

During the Prime Minister’s Question, the Prime Minister adopted a calm demeanour that resonated with many across the country. In his remarks, Starmer announced that his government would no longer be cooperating with the Reform UK, marking a significant shift in political strategy.

The Prime Minister also emphasised that his administration would prioritise stabilising and repairing the British economy in the coming months.

Starmer further revealed that he had previously been working with the Conservative Party to draft a joint bill. However, the proposal has since been cancelled following strong negative public reaction. Despite this setback, he expressed hope that both parties could eventually reach a compromise and cooperate on future legislation.

Notably, Jeremy Corbyn and Shabana Mahmood constantly grilled the Prime Minister during the recent PMQs, showcasing fractures in the nation's left wing.
